A hub is the meeting point that ties a Direct Connect community together. Running one means you provide that space: people connect to your hub to chat, search, and find each other for transfers. This section walks through choosing software, setting it up, moderating, and listing your hub.

## What you need

- A machine that stays on: a home server, a spare PC, or a VPS.
- A reachable address: an open port (with port forwarding if you are behind a router), or a server with a public IP.
- A name and short description for your hub.
- Basic comfort administering your operating system.

:::warning[Be ready for the upkeep]
Hub software is easy to install, and a small hub is running in minutes. The work grows with the hub: moderation, configuration, and the right security settings take more time as your hub gets bigger. Running a hub well needs basic IT-security knowledge for your platform (Windows or Linux).
:::
